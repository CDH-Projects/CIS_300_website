Grip Pressure

One of the easiest ways to lose control of the club is by holding it too tightly. A lighter grip helps reduce tension in your hands, wrists, and forearms, which can lead to a smoother and more natural swing. You want control, but not stiffness.

A good checkpoint is to hold the club firmly enough that it feels secure, but lightly enough that your swing still feels relaxed. If your arms feel tense before impact, there is a good chance your grip pressure is too strong.

Pre-Shot Routine

A consistent pre-shot routine can help settle your nerves and make your swing more repeatable. Instead of stepping up to the ball differently every time, build a sequence you can trust. That might mean picking your target, taking one practice swing, setting your feet, and then swinging.

The routine does not need to be complicated. The point is to create something simple and repeatable so your body and mind feel prepared before every shot.

Balance Through the Swing

Balance plays a major role in clean contact and overall consistency. If you are falling backward, leaning too far forward, or finishing off balance, your swing path and contact point can become unreliable.

Focus on staying centered during the backswing and finishing in a controlled position. A balanced finish is often a sign that your tempo was under control and your body stayed connected through the shot.
